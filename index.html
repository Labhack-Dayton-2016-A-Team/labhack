The Air Force Hackathon

and Mini-Conference 2014

Mission Accomplished!


We were unable to host LabHack 2015. However, we are looking for a few good challenges for LabHack 2016.Labhack 2016 challenge submission form.

Thank you for attending LabHack, the first Air Force hackathon. We hope you can join us at future LabHacks!

This was a chance for participants to show off programming, design and data science skills by building solutions to real-world challenges that AFRL researchers encounter every day. We provided the food, location, big network pipelines and challenges. We had cash prizes, workshops, talks, swag and fun events along the way. The event was open to the public.

  • What: A hackathon on data and sensors
  • When: Saturday, 25 October 2014 - Sunday, 26 October 2014
  • Where: WBI Tec^Edge Innovation and Collaboration Center, Dayton, OH
  • Why: To learn something new, build something awesome and have fun!
  • Who: This event was open to the public. Anyone, military or civilian, could participate.
For more questions and answers, see our FAQ.

Winning Teams

AFRL Prize:
  • 1st ($600 + 1 year silver subscription to Github): Eye Movement - EOG Data Display (Source Code)
  • 2nd ($300 + 1 year bronze subscription to Github): Physiological Data Visualization (Source Code)
  • 3rd ($150 + 6 months bronze subscription to Github): Jammer Detection (Source Code)
Community Prize: Each team member gets to choose O'Reilly book or Dayton Dragons Gear
  • 1st: HUMAN Laboratory Formal Study 1 Analysis (Source Code)
  • 2nd: Personnel Monitoring with Lightweight UDP Datagrams for Intermittent Connections (Source Code)
  • 3rd: AFRL Human Laboratory Challenge 2 (Source Code)
Judges Award: Gave two Dayton Dragons hats to Health Monitoring, Alert System, and Spotty Connection(Source Code).
